How to Calculate Kitchen Equipment Costs When Opening a Restaurant

July 22, 2026

Kitchen equipment is one of the biggest investment items when opening a new restaurant or cafe. Proper budgeting directly affects your business's cash flow in its first years.

Factors That Affect Equipment Cost

The size of the venue, the menu type (a pizza restaurant and a cafe have very different equipment needs), whether the equipment is standard or custom-made, and the grade of stainless steel are the key factors determining total cost.

How Much of the Budget Should Go to Equipment?

Industry averages suggest professional kitchen setup can make up a significant share of the total opening investment; many owners underestimate this item and face additional costs later. Setting a separate, realistic equipment budget prevents surprise expenses.

Watch Out for Hidden Costs

Installation, shipping, electrical/plumbing adaptation, ventilation connections and staff training are items frequently overlooked. Always ask whether these are included when getting a quote.

Choosing the Right Supplier Saves Money

Buying directly from the manufacturer removes intermediary costs and eases your budget. Suppliers offering a 2-year warranty and technical support also reduce your long-term maintenance costs.
