Maintenance and Cleaning of Stainless Steel Kitchen Equipment

July 22, 2026

Although stainless steel equipment is durable, staining, corrosion and performance loss can occur without regular maintenance.

Daily Maintenance

Wetting the surface and wiping it with a soft cloth or sponge and warm soapy water after each use prevents bacterial growth and permanent staining. Wiping in the direction of the steel grain, rather than circular motions, protects the surface.

Weekly Polishing

Natural polishing with vinegar or lemon juice effectively removes limescale and grease buildup. Letting it sit for a few minutes before rinsing is enough.

Monthly Professional Care

For heavily used equipment, a monthly professional polish or polishing product application preserves the original look of the surface and removes micro-scratches.

Mistakes to Avoid

Using steel wool or abrasive sponges scratches the surface, creating a foothold for corrosion. Heavy chlorine-based cleaners can also damage stainless steel over the long term.
