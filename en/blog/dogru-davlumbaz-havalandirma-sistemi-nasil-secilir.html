How to Choose the Right Hood and Ventilation System for a Restaurant Kitchen

July 22, 2026

The hood and ventilation system is one of the most critical components of a kitchen, both for comfort and legal compliance.

Sizing for Cooking Capacity

Hood selection should be based on the number of cooking stations, cooking intensity, and smoke/odor density. Undersizing causes smoke buildup in the kitchen and reduces staff comfort.

Material and Filter Quality

Hoods are usually made of stainless steel; AISI 316 grade is preferred in kitchens with heavy flame use. Grease filters must be cleaned regularly, otherwise the duct system risks clogging.

System Components

A complete ventilation system consists of the hood, ductwork, exhaust fan, chimney and a fresh-air fan. If any of these components is missing or undersized, the efficiency of the whole system drops.

Fire Safety

Integrating automatic fire detection and suppression systems into the hood line is vital in industrial kitchens for handling potential grease fires.
