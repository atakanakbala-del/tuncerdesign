Criteria to Consider When Choosing Industrial Kitchen Equipment

July 22, 2026

The right equipment choice directly affects your kitchen's efficiency, hygiene and long-term costs.

Capacity Matching Your Menu

Equipment capacity should be determined by your daily service volume and peak-hour demand. Undersized capacity causes service delays, while oversized capacity wastes energy and space.

Stainless Steel Grade

Kitchens with heavy flame use should use AISI 316 grade stainless steel, while AISI 304 grade is sufficient for standard kitchens. This grade difference directly affects corrosion resistance and product lifespan.

Certification and Standards

CE and ISO 9001 certified equipment guarantees compliance with both safety and quality standards. These certificates may also be mandatory for businesses serving international markets.

Service and Spare Parts Support

Before purchasing, always ask about the supplier's warranty period, technical support line and spare parts lead time. A fast response during a breakdown minimizes business losses.
